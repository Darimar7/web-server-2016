Corals From Puerto Rico And the Caribbean

Corals are live animals, and not rocks or plants as many people think since these exhibits similar characteristics. They are part of the phylum Cnidaria and the class Anthozoa; which means, they are family of anemones and jellyfishes. Although there are a variety of corals in the Caribbean, in the Pacific there are many more species. For example, in the Caribbean there are only 2 species of Acroporids, while in the Pacific there are hundreds of species of Acroporids.

Where can you find corals? Environmental Conditions.

Corals are normally found in relatively shallow water where sunlight comes in temperatures ranging from 70-85F. However, there are corals in cold waters and in deep waters as well.

Reproductive methods Sexual and Asexual.

Corals reproduce sexually by fertilization of gametes that results in the planula (coral larvae) and also asexually by fragmentation. Corals are fed by a mutualistic relationship with photosynthetic algae called zooxanthella.
